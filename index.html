Yuxin Wang is currently a researcher at Huawei Hong Kong Research Center (HKRC). He received his Ph.D. in Computer Science from Hong Kong Baptist University, supervised by Prof. Xiaowen Chu, and his B.Eng. in Electrical Engineering from Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST).

His research focuses on AI infrastructure and systems optimization:

  • Infra for AI: efficient and reliable systems for large-scale model training and serving, including LLM serving optimization (e.g., MoE disaggregated inference, elastic expert parallelism) and fault-tolerant distributed training.
  • AI for Infra: leveraging LLMs to automate infrastructure tasks such as kernel code generation and system performance optimization.

💻 Open Source Projects

BurstGPT

The first real-world trace dataset of LLM serving workloads from Azure OpenAI GPT services. Contains 10.31M traces collected over 213 days. Published at KDD 2025.

LLM Serving Dataset KDD 2025

AFD & Elastic AFD (vLLM)

Attn-FFN Disaggregation for MoE model inference in vLLM, decoupling Attention and FFN/expert phases for efficient DeepSeek-v3/R1 serving. Elastic AFD further enables dynamic scaling during traffic spikes and node failures.

RFC: AFD RFC: Elastic AFD

Elastic and Fault-Tolerant EP (vLLM)

Process-level fault tolerance for vLLM distributed inference. Enables fast local recovery (seconds vs. minutes) from GPU/network failures in MoE hybrid-parallel deployments, with fault detection, status reporting, and elastic EP-based device isolation.
